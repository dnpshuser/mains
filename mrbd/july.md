The expansion of tourism infrastructure in ecologically sensitive zones

The Intent (Enunciate/Explain): Building mega highways, hotels, and ropeways in mountainous regions boosts the local economy, creates tourism jobs, and improves connectivity for remote villages.

The Counter-Challenge (Counter): This rapid, uncontrolled construction causes massive environmental damage, leading to frequent landslides, deforestation, and flash floods that endanger local communities.

The Resolution (Resolve): The government needs to mandate strict environmental impact assessments (EIA) and shift toward sustainable, low-impact eco-tourism models.

[Without seeing hint/facts]
Tourism is an emerging sector which has become an important pillar to make India a 5 trillion economy. We must expand tourism infrastructure across the country. However, ecologically sensitive zones like Himalyas, Nilgiris Hills, Andaman and Nicobar islands, etc, are severely impacted with extinction of flora and fauna. Thus reconciling economic growth imperatives with biodiversity protection and sustainable ecology mandates a robust policy to enable travelling through fragile environments.
