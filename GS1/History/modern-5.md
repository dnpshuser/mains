### Q83. What is the fundamental difference between Revolutionaries of Phase I & Phase II ??
- *Intro:*
  - While both phases aimed at the armed overthrow of the British, they differed significantly in their organizational structure, social outlook, and ideology.
- *Body:*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Ideological Shift}}}$: Phase I was driven by romantic nationalism and religious pride, while Phase II was heavily influenced by Marxism and Socialism — *Example: The shift from 'Yugantar' (Phase I) to the 'Hindustan Socialist Republican Association' (Phase II).*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Organization Type}}}$: Phase I relied on secretive, isolated 'secret societies,' whereas Phase II moved toward wider political organizations — *Example: Anushilan Samiti vs. HSRA.*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Goal of Violence}}}$: Phase I focused on individual heroics and assassinations to terrify officials; Phase II aimed at mass revolution — *Example: Killing of Magistrate Kingsford vs. Bombing the Legislative Assembly to 'make the deaf hear'.*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Social Base}}}$: Phase I was largely restricted to the educated upper-caste youth; Phase II included workers, peasants, and students in a broader way — *Example: Bhagat Singh’s efforts to link with the Naujawan Bharat Sabha.*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Secular Outlook}}}$: Phase II was more explicitly secular compared to the religious symbolism of Phase I — *Example: The partnership between Ashfaqullah Khan and Ram Prasad Bismil.*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{International Context}}}$: Phase I was inspired by Irish struggles; Phase II was inspired by the Russian Revolution — *Example: Study of Lenin’s ideas by later revolutionaries.*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Vision for Future}}}$: Phase I mostly focused on independence; Phase II envisioned a socialist republic with no exploitation — *Example: The HSRA manifesto 'The Philosophy of the Bomb'.*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Publicity Tactics}}}$: Phase II used court trials as a platform to spread their message, unlike the purely clandestine work of Phase I — *Example: Bhagat Singh and Dutt's statements during their trial.*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Role of Women}}}$: Phase II saw much more active and combatant participation of women — *Example: Pritilata Waddedar and Kalpana Datta in the Chittagong Raid.*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Weaponry}}}$: Phase I used mostly small arms; Phase II attempted larger military raids and sabotage — *Example: The raid on the Chittagong Armoury (1930).*
- *Conclusion:*
  - The evolution from Phase I to Phase II reflects the maturation of the revolutionary mind, moving from individual martyrdom to a systematic vision for a new social order.

### Q84. What were the salient features of Nehru report ?
- *Intro:*
  - Drafted in 1928, the Nehru Report was India’s first major attempt by Indians to draft a constitutional framework, serving as a challenge to British claims of Indian incompetence.
- *Body:*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Dominion Status}}}$: It demanded self-government within the British Empire, similar to other white colonies — *Example: Aimed at achieving status equal to Canada or Australia.*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Fundamental Rights}}}$: Proposed a list of 19 rights to be guaranteed to every citizen — *Example: Freedom of speech, assembly, and right to form unions.*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Universal Adult Suffrage}}}$: Advocated for the right to vote for all adults, regardless of gender or property — *Example: A radical departure from the limited British franchise.*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Rejecting Separate Electorates}}}$: Proposed joint electorates with reservation of seats for minorities in proportion to population — *Example: Attempted to curb communal divisions.*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Secular State}}}$: Asserted that the state should have no official religion and should not interfere in religious matters — *Example: Promoting a composite Indian identity.*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Federal Structure}}}$: Suggested a federation with residuary powers resting with the Center — *Example: Aimed at creating a strong, unified India.*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Linguistic Provinces}}}$: Proposed redrawing provincial boundaries based on language to improve administration — *Example: Demanded the creation of Sindh and Odisha.*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Bicameral Legislature}}}$: Proposed a parliament consisting of a Senate and a House of Representatives — *Example: Modeled on Western democratic structures.*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Responsible Government}}}$: The executive was to be fully responsible to the legislature at both the Center and provinces — *Example: Abolishing the absolute veto power of the Governor.*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Judiciary Independence}}}$: Proposed a Supreme Court to act as the guardian of the Constitution — *Example: Ensuring legal check on executive overreach.*
- *Conclusion:*
  - Although rejected by the British and some communal factions, the Nehru Report remains a seminal document that laid the foundations for the modern Indian Constitution.

### Q92. What are the salient features of Govt of India Act 1935 ? [answer in 15-20 points]
- *Intro:*
  - The 1935 Act was the most comprehensive and complex constitutional document produced by the British for India, serving as a blueprint for the future.
- *Body:*

  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Bicameral Federal Legislature}}}$: Created a Council of State and a Federal Assembly.

  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Expansion of Franchise}}}$: Increased the voting population to about 10% of the total population.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Separation of Burma}}}$: Burma was formally detached from British India.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{New Provinces}}}$: Created the provinces of Sindh and Odisha on linguistic/communal grounds.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Abolition of Council of India}}}$: Replaced the Secretary of State’s council with a set of advisers.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Extended Electorates}}}$: Continued separate electorates for Muslims, Sikhs, and now Dalits and labor.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Safeguards and Veto}}}$: The Governor-General retained 'special responsibilities' and a veto over bills.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Division of Subjects}}}$: Created three lists—Federal, Provincial, and Concurrent.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Railway Authority}}}$: Created an autonomous body to manage railways, insulating them from Indian politics.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Residuary Powers}}}$: Powers not mentioned in any list were given to the Governor-General.
- *Conclusion:*
  - While the Congress called it a "Charter of Slavery," the Act provided the structural framework upon which independent India's constitution was built.

- *Body:*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Failure of Cripps Mission}}}$: The refusal of the British to grant immediate transfer of power led to a deadlock — *Example: Refusal to share defense responsibilities.*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Economic Distress}}}$: High inflation and shortage of food during WWII caused mass suffering — *Example: Rise in prices of salt and kerosene.*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Fear of Japanese Invasion}}}$: Gandhi felt that only a free India could defend itself effectively against Japan — *Example: British retreat from Malaya and Burma.*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Desire for Decisive Action}}}$: The youth and masses were becoming impatient with long constitutional waits — *Example: Gandhi’s shift from 'passive resistance' to 'Do or Die'.*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Bose's Influence}}}$: The news of the INA and Bose’s activities abroad energized the Indian mind.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{British Arrogance}}}$: Attlee's government showed no signs of realizing that the empire was over.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Loss of Faith in British Ethics}}}$: British "scorched earth" policies in Bengal during the war angered the public.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Mobilization of All Classes}}}$: The realization that the British were only listening to the language of force.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Gandhi’s Radicalization}}}$: For the first time, Gandhi was willing to tolerate a certain level of anarchy for the goal of freedom.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{International Pressure}}}$: American and Chinese pressure on Britain to solve the Indian problem.
- *Conclusion:*
  - Quit India was a "successful failure"—it was crushed, but it made it clear that the British could no longer rule India by consent.

### Q95. What were the impacts of Quit India Movement ?
- *Intro:*
  - Though suppressed by brute force, the 'August Revolution' shifted the political balance permanently in favor of independence.
- *Body:*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{End of British Prestige}}}$: Shattered the myth that the British Raj was benevolent or permanent.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Parallel Governments}}}$: In several districts, British authority was totally displaced — *Example: Prati Sarkar in Satara and Jatiya Sarkar in Tamluk.*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Mass Participation}}}$: Involved peasants, students, and workers on an unprecedented scale — *Example: Massive involvement of youth in the underground.*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Underground Activity}}}$: Leaders like Jayaprakash Narayan and Aruna Asaf Ali kept the flame alive from secret hideouts — *Example: Congress Radio by Usha Mehta.*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Shift in Military Loyalty}}}$: Realization that Indian soldiers might not always remain loyal — *Example: Refusal to fire on crowds in some instances.*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Psychological Breakthrough}}}$: Instilled a sense of fearlessness and 'Do or Die' attitude in the common man.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{International Spotlight}}}$: Forced the world to notice the intensity of Indian nationalism during a global war.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Final Warning}}}$: It acted as a final notice to the British that they must prepare to leave.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Weakened Bureaucracy}}}$: The administration was severely strained and exhausted by the end of 1944.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Paving way for INA}}}$: The internal revolt created a climate that was receptive to Subhash Bose’s external invasion.
- *Conclusion:*
  - Quit India ensured that the post-war negotiations would be about the 'timing' of independence, not its 'possibility'.

### Q96. What were the contributions of Subash Chandra Bose in freedom struggle ?
- *Intro:*
  - "Netaji" was the most dynamic leader who introduced the element of "militarized nationalism" to the struggle, believing that freedom must be "taken" not "requested."
- *Body:*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Forward Bloc Formation}}}$: Created a radical leftist faction within Congress to push for faster action — *Example: Founded in 1939.*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{President of Congress}}}$: Twice elected, pushing for a more assertive anti-imperialist stand — *Example: Haripura (1938) and Tripuri (1939) sessions.*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Indian National Army (INA)}}}$, **Reorganization**: Transformed the INA into a formidable fighting force in SE Asia — *Example: Recruitments from POWs and civilians in Malaya.*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Arzi Hukumat-e-Azad Hind}}}$: Established a provisional government-in-exile recognized by nine world powers — *Example: Headquartered in Singapore.*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Psychological Warfare}}}$: His radio broadcasts from Tokyo and Berlin kept the morale of Indians high — *Example: Azad Hind Radio.*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{National Planning}}}$: Pioneered the concept of planned industrialization for a free India — *Example: Appointment of the National Planning Committee (1938).*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Women's Empowerment}}}$: Created the first all-women combat regiment in Asia — *Example: Rani of Jhansi Regiment led by Lakshmi Sahgal.*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Communal Unity}}}$: His army was the perfect model of secularism — *Example: Officers from all religions (Dhillon, Sehgal, Shah Nawaz).*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Slogans of Freedom}}}$: Gave India its most powerful rallying cries — *Example: "Jai Hind" and "Give me blood, I will give you freedom".*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{INA Trials Impact}}}$: The subsequent trials of his officers triggered the final breakdown of British authority in the Indian army.
- *Conclusion:*
  - Bose provided the "military hammer" that eventually broke the steel frame of the British Raj.

### Q97. How did demand of Pakistan rise in India ?
- *Intro:*
  - The demand for Pakistan was the culmination of sectarian fears, political miscalculations, and colonial encouragement.
- *Body:*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Two-Nation Theory}}}$: The intellectual assertion that Hindus and Muslims were two separate nations — *Example: Propagated by Sir Syed and later Iqbal.*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Illahabad Address (1930)}}}$: Sir Muhammad Iqbal proposed a separate North-Western Muslim state.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{The Name 'Pakistan'}}}$: Coined by Choudhry Rahmat Ali in 1933 in his pamphlet 'Now or Never'.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{1937 Election Results}}}$: The League’s poor performance led Jinnah to shift to radical communal mobilization.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Fear of 'Hindu Raj'}}}$: The League portrayed Congress rule as a threat to Muslim culture and religion.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Lahore Resolution (1940)}}}$: The formal demand for "Independent States" for Muslims in the NW and East — *Example: Often called the Pakistan Resolution.*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{World War 2 Opportunity}}}$: The British gave the League a "veto" over any constitutional progress as a reward for war support.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Cripps Mission Failure}}}$: The mission’s hint at provincial secession encouraged separatist ideas.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Jinnah's Leadership}}}$: His 'Great Solo Act' of branding the League as the sole representative of Muslims.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Communal Violence}}}$: Riots in the 1940s made the idea of peaceful coexistence seem impossible.
- *Conclusion:*
  - What started as a "bargaining counter" for Jinnah eventually became an unalterable political reality.

### Q98. What were the proposals of Cripps Mission ?
- *Intro:*
  - Sent in March 1942, the mission aimed to secure Indian cooperation during the most critical phase of World War II.
- *Body:*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Dominion Status}}}$: Promised self-government within the empire after the war.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Constituent Assembly}}}$: Proposed an Indian-elected body to frame a new constitution.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Secession Clause}}}$: Any province not willing to accept the new constitution could form its own separate union — *Example: A "blueprint for partition".*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Interim Defense}}}$: The responsibility for India's defense was to remain with the British during the war.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Participation of Princes}}}$: Princely states were given the choice to join the union or stay separate.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Treaty Negotiation}}}$: A treaty was to be signed between Britain and the Constituent Assembly to transfer power.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{No Immediate Power}}}$: The executive council remained under the Viceroy's control without any real transfer of authority.
- *Conclusion:*
  - Gandhi famously called it a "Post-dated cheque on a failing bank."

### Q99. Why Cripps Mission failed ?
- *Intro:*
  - The mission failed because it offered too little in the present and too much (division) in the future.
- *Body:*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Refusal of Cabinet Form}}}$: The British refused to give the Executive Council the powers of a cabinet.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Defense Responsibility}}}$: Indians were denied any real control over the defense budget or strategy.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Balkanization Fear}}}$: The right of provinces to secede was seen by Congress as a threat to Indian unity.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Viceroy's Veto}}}$: The British insisted that the Viceroy's absolute veto would remain intact during the war.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Muslim League Rejection}}}$: The League rejected it because there was no explicit mention of Pakistan.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Post-dated nature}}}$: All real promises were for "after the war," which Indians did not trust.
- *Conclusion:*
  - The failure of Cripps directly triggered the Quit India Movement.

### Q100. What caused famine in India during world war 2 ?
- *Intro:*
  - The Bengal Famine of 1943 killed nearly 3 million people and was a "policy-driven" man-made disaster.
- *Body:*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Burma Occupation}}}$: The Japanese takeover of Burma stopped the vital import of rice to India.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Scorched Earth Policy}}}$: British destroyed boats and grain in Bengal to prevent them from falling to the Japanese.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Military Priority}}}$: Food was diverted to feed the British Indian Army and Allied troops overseas.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Hoarding and Speculation}}}$: Local traders hoarded grain in expectation of further price rises during the war.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Administrative Indifference}}}$: Churchill’s cabinet refused to divert ships or grain from the UK to help India.
- *Conclusion:*
  - The famine was the ultimate price India paid for being a subordinate unit of the British war effort.
