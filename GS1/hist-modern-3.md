  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Devadasi System}}}$: Exploitation of women under the guise of religious service in temples — *Example: Prevalent in parts of South India and Odisha.*

  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Infrastructure for Control}}}$: Rapid expansion of Railways and Telegraphs for military movement — *Example: Over 25,000 miles of track laid by the end of the century.*

  - Post-1857 reforms marked the transition from "Merchant Rule" to "Imperial Rule," characterized by social conservatism and military vigilance.

  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Administrative Changes}}}$: It resulted in the abolition of the Doctrine of Lapse — *Example: Protecting the interests of the princely states.*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Focus on Reform}}}$: It made Indian leaders realize that modern organization was needed instead of feudal leadership — *Example: The rise of modern political associations post-1857.*
- *Conclusion:*
  - 1857 was a military failure but a political success that provided the emotional and organizational seeds for the modern nationalist movement.

### Q54. What caused the Surat split?
- *Intro:*

- *Body:*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Difference in Goals}}}$: Moderates wanted self-government within the empire, while Extremists demanded "Swaraj" or independence — *Example: Difference between "Dominion status" and absolute rule.*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Extent of Swadeshi}}}$: Extremists wanted to expand the Swadeshi movement into a full boycott of all British institutions — *Example: Boycott of government schools and courts.*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Method of Struggle}}}$: Moderates adhered to constitutionalism, while Extremists favored passive resistance and mass mobilization — *Example: Direct action vs. petitions.*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Geographical Spread}}}$: Extremists wanted the anti-partition movement to spread across India, while Moderates wanted to keep it in Bengal — *Example: Tilak’s desire to internationalize the struggle.*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Presidential Conflict}}}$: Disagreement over the choice of Congress president for the Surat session — *Example: Extremists proposing Lajpat Rai vs. Moderates choosing Rash Behari Ghosh.*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{The Venue Change}}}$: Shifting the session from Nagpur (an extremist stronghold) to Surat — *Example: Moderates attempting to isolate Tilak.*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Lack of Trust}}}$: Both sides believed the other was being manipulated by the British — *Example: Moderates fearing government repression.*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{The 1906 Resolutions}}}$: Tensions over whether to re-affirm the Swadeshi and Boycott resolutions passed in Calcutta — *Example: Attempt by Moderates to dilute the agenda.*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Physical Altercation}}}$: The session ended in chaos and shoe-throwing — *Example: Total breakdown of order on the Congress floor.*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Government Strategy}}}$: The British policy of "rallying the moderates" and suppressing extremists encouraged the split — *Example: Minto-Morley's divide and rule within Congress.*
- *Conclusion:*
  - The Surat split was a "national calamity" as it weakened the movement and allowed the British to arrest extremist leaders like Tilak.
