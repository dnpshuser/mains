### Q1. What is suo-moto congnizance of matters ? How it has helped judiciary to practise judicial activism in India ? 

- **Intro:**
  - Suo-moto cognizance refers to the power of the High Courts (**Article 226**) and the Supreme Court (**Article 32**) to take up a case on their own motion, without a formal petition. This is an extension of the **Epistolary Jurisdiction** and has been the primary vehicle for **Judicial Activism** in India.

- **Body:**
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Dilution of Locus Standi}}}$: Suo-moto action allows the court to represent the "voiceless" who cannot approach the court due to poverty or lack of awareness.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Article 142 (Complete Justice)}}}$: The SC uses its inherent powers under **Art 142** to pass orders in suo-moto cases to fill legislative gaps and provide immediate relief.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Checking Executive Inaction}}}$: It allows the judiciary to step in when the executive fails to perform its duty — *Example: SC's intervention during the 2021 Oxygen crisis during the pandemic.*
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Human Rights Defense}}}$: The court has historically used news reports to take cognizance of custodial deaths, bonded labor, and police brutality (*Example: Sunil Batra v. Delhi Administration*).
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Environmental Protection}}}$: Much of India's environmental law evolved through suo-moto/PIL routes, such as the "Green Bench" for Delhi's air quality or protection of the Ganga.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Sita Soren v. Union of India (2024)}}}$: In this recent landmark, the SC proactively revisited its 1998 judgment to ensure that parliamentary privilege (**Art 105/194**) does not protect bribery.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Women's Safety}}}$: Following incidents like the Nirbhaya case or Manipur violence, the court has taken suo-moto note to issue guidelines for women's safety and compensation.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Prison Reforms}}}$: The SC has taken cognizance of prison overcrowding and the plight of undertrials, issuing directions for the constitution of Under Trial Review Committees.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Electoral Transparency}}}$: The judiciary's proactive stance on candidate disclosures and the "NOTA" option was often bolstered by judicial interventions.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Child Rights}}}$: Taking up matters of child trafficking, child labor, and nutrition in schools (Mid-day Meal monitoring).
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Guardian of the Constitution}}}$: Suo-moto action ensures that constitutional morality is upheld even if the political branches choose silence on sensitive issues.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Public Accountability}}}$: By monitoring investigations in corruption cases (e.g., 2G, Coal Scam), the court ensures that high-profile investigations are not compromised.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Filling Legislative Vacuums}}}$: Until laws are made, the SC's suo-moto/PIL guidelines act as the law of the land (*Example: Vishaka Guidelines*).
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Limitations (Judicial Overreach)}}}$: While helpful, it is criticized when it interferes in administrative domains like policy making or taxing, potentially violating the **Separation of Powers**.

- **Conclusion:**
  - Suo-moto cognizance has transformed the Indian judiciary from a "passive adjudicator" to a "proactive protector." However, the "self-restraint" of judges is essential to ensure that activism doesn't turn into an unconstitutional overreach.

#### Q1. Suo-moto Cognizance & Judicial Activism
*   **Intro:** Suo-moto cognizance (under Art 32/226) is the power of courts to take up cases on their own, acting as the primary vehicle for judicial activism in India.
*   **Body Keywords:**
    1.  Dilution of Locus Standi
    2.  Article 142 (Complete Justice)
    3.  Checking Executive Inaction (e.g., Oxygen crisis)
    4.  Human Rights Defense (*Sunil Batra*)
    5.  Environmental Protection (Green Bench)
    6.  *Sita Soren v. UoI (2024)* (Parliamentary Privilege)
    7.  Women's Safety (Nirbhaya)
    8.  Prison Reforms
    9.  Electoral Transparency (NOTA)
    10. Child Rights
    11. Guardian of the Constitution
    12. Public Accountability (2G, Coal Scam)
    13. Filling Legislative Vacuums (*Vishaka Guidelines*)
    14. Limitations (Judicial Overreach)
*   **Conclusion:** Suo-moto action has made the judiciary a "proactive protector," but judicial self-restraint is crucial to avoid overreach.

#### Q2. Judiciary as a Court of Record
*   **Intro:** Under Article 129 (SC) and Article 215 (HC), the higher judiciary is designated as a "Court of Record," a status with two key implications: its records have evidentiary value and it can punish for its own contempt.
*   **Body Keywords:**
    1.  **Evidentiary Value:** Judgments are recorded for perpetual memory and testimony.
    2.  **Precedential Authority:** Lower courts are bound by the decisions of higher courts, ensuring legal consistency.
    3.  **Power to Punish for Contempt:** This power is essential to maintain the dignity, authority, and independence of the judiciary.
    4.  **Upholding Rule of Law:** Ensures that judicial orders are respected and implemented by all, including the government.
*   **Conclusion:** The status of a "Court of Record" is a constitutional safeguard that ensures the authority of the judiciary and the stability of the legal system.

#### Q3. Judicial Review vs. Judicial Overreach
*   **Intro:** Judicial review is the power of the judiciary to examine the constitutionality of legislative and executive actions. When this power transgresses into the domain of the legislature or executive, it is termed judicial overreach.
*   **Body Keywords:**
    1.  **Judicial Review (Legitimate):** Upholding Basic Structure (*Kesavananda*), Protecting FRs, Checking arbitrariness.
    2.  **Judicial Overreach (Problematic):** Entering policy domain (e.g., fixing fuel prices), Legislating from the bench (e.g., banning firecrackers), Interfering in administration.
    3.  **Separation of Powers:** Overreach violates the delicate balance of power between the three organs of the state.
*   **Conclusion:** While judicial review is a bulwark of democracy, judicial overreach can undermine the separation of powers. A fine line of "judicial self-restraint" is essential.

#### Q4. Judicial Accountability
*   **Intro:** While the judiciary holds immense power, the mechanisms for its accountability are often seen as weak and internal, raising questions about transparency.
*   **Body Keywords:**
    1.  **Immense Power:** Judicial Review, Contempt Power, Basic Structure Doctrine.
    2.  **Limited Accountability:**
        *   **Impeachment (Art 124(4)):** A complex political process that has never succeeded.
        *   **Internal Mechanisms:** In-house procedures for inquiry are often opaque.
        *   **Contempt Power:** Can be used to stifle criticism.
        *   **Lack of Transparency:** The Collegium system of appointments lacks transparency.
*   **Conclusion:** There is a need to balance judicial independence with accountability, possibly through a more transparent appointment process and a robust judicial standards and accountability law.

#### Q5. Judicial Legislation & Separation of Powers
*   **Intro:** When the judiciary, in its verdicts, creates new laws or detailed guidelines, it is often termed "judicial legislation." This practice, while sometimes necessary to fill legislative vacuums, raises concerns about the violation of the doctrine of separation of powers.
*   **Body Keywords:**
    1.  **Parliament's Process:** Complex, involves debate, public consultation, and political consensus.
    2.  **Judicial Process:** Based on arguments between two parties, lacks broad public consultation.
    3.  **Example (*Vishaka v. State of Rajasthan*):** The SC laid down detailed guidelines to prevent sexual harassment at the workplace, which acted as law until Parliament enacted a statute.
    4.  **Justification:** Done to protect fundamental rights when the legislature fails to act.
    5.  **Violation of Separation of Powers:** Critics argue that law-making is the exclusive domain of the legislature.
*   **Conclusion:** Judicial legislation is a double-edged sword. It can be a powerful tool for social justice but must be used with restraint to avoid upsetting the constitutional balance.

#### Q6. Judicial Activism & Nullifying Laws
*   **Intro:** The power of the judiciary to nullify a law passed by Parliament is a core component of judicial review. The rising trend of judicial activism sees courts using this power more proactively to protect constitutional values.
*   **Body Keywords:**
    1.  **Grounds for Nullification:** Violation of Fundamental Rights, against Basic Structure, legislative incompetence.
    2.  **Example (NJAC Act):** The SC struck down the 99th Constitutional Amendment and the NJAC Act, holding that it violated the independence of the judiciary, a basic feature.
    3.  **Example (Section 66A, IT Act):** Struck down in *Shreya Singhal* case for violating freedom of speech.
    4.  **Justification:** The judiciary acts as the ultimate guardian of the Constitution.
    5.  **Criticism:** Can lead to a confrontation between the judiciary and the legislature, and is sometimes seen as undemocratic.
*   **Conclusion:** While Parliament represents the will of the people, the judiciary represents the will of the Constitution. Judicial activism in nullifying unconstitutional laws is a vital check on legislative overreach.
