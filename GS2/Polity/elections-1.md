### Q1. How One Nation One Election violates federalism ?

- **Intro:**
  - "One Nation One Election" (ONOE) refers to holding simultaneous elections to the Lok Sabha and all State Legislative Assemblies. While it aims for administrative efficiency, critics argue it infringes upon the federal structure—a "Basic Structure" of the Constitution.

- **Body:**
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Undermining State Autonomy}}}$: Every state assembly has its own 5-year term (**Article 172**); forcing them to align with the Lok Sabha term violates their independent democratic mandate.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Art 174 & Art 85 Conflict}}}$: The Governor/President’s power to dissolve the house prematurely would be curtailed if elections must follow a rigid national schedule.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Marginalization of Regional Issues}}}$: Simultaneous polls tend to nationalize the discourse, overshadowing specific regional, linguistic, and local concerns with national "waves."
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Voter Behavior Bias}}}$: Studies suggest that when elections are held together, voters are more likely to vote for the same party at both levels, disadvantaging regional parties.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Standardization of Terms}}}$: Federalism thrives on the diversity of political cycles; "syncing" them treats states as administrative units of the Centre rather than sovereign entities.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Issue of Mid-term Collapse}}}$: If a state government falls due to a "No-Confidence Motion," ONOE would require either President's Rule or a "short-term" assembly, both of which are anti-democratic.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{President’s Rule (Article 356)}}}$, **Potential Misuse**: There is a fear that the Centre might use Art 356 more frequently to "align" state election dates with the national cycle.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Resource Concentration}}}$: Larger national parties with massive budgets have a disproportionate advantage in a single, massive election cycle compared to resource-constrained regional parties.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Constitutional Amendment Requirements}}}$: ONOE requires amending **Articles 83, 85, 172, 174, and 356**, necessitating ratification by half the states—a process that currently lacks federal consensus.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Impact on Local Bodies}}}$: Extending ONOE to include Panchayats and Municipalities (**Part IX/IXA**) would further centralize the democratic process.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Accountability Lapse}}}$: Frequent elections keep politicians on their toes; ONOE might lead to a 5-year "accountability holiday" for elected representatives.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Diverse Political Mandates}}}$: Federalism allows for different parties to rule at the Centre and in states; ONOE puts this "check and balance" at risk.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Kovind Committee Report 2024}}}$: The high-level committee recently recommended ONOE, but dissenting voices highlight the structural risk to the "Multi-layered" democracy of India.

- **Conclusion:**
  - ONOE may offer "Efficiency," but Federalism offers "Representation." Any move towards simultaneous polls must protect the constitutional independence of states to ensure that the "Union of States" remains a reality.

### Q2. What are the advantages of One Nation One Election ?

- **Intro:**
  - The proposal for simultaneous elections (ONOE) has gained traction as a solution to India's "permanent election mode." Proponents argue it would streamline governance, reduce costs, and focus on long-term development.

- **Body:**
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Reduction in Election Expenditure}}}$: ONOE would significantly lower the massive public and private spending on repeated election cycles.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Governance Continuity}}}$: Frequent implementation of the **Model Code of Conduct (MCC)** leads to "Policy Paralysis" as new projects cannot be launched; ONOE would limit this to once in 5 years.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Administrative Efficiency}}}$: Massive deployment of security forces (CAPFs) and government teachers for election duty disrupts regular law and order and education.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Focus on Long-term Policy}}}$: Without the pressure of a "semi-final" election every few months, governments can take bold, necessary but unpopular decisions.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Voter Fatigue Reduction}}}$: Holding all elections at once can increase voter turnout by reducing the frequency of trips to the polling booth.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Check on Populism}}}$: Frequent elections often lead to "competitive populism" and short-term "freebies" that strain the fiscal health of the nation.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Enhanced Stability}}}$: Mechanisms like "Constructive Vote of No-Confidence" (as in Germany) could be introduced to ensure governments complete their 5-year terms.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Social Harmony}}}$: Elections often trigger communal or caste-based polarization; reducing their frequency might help maintain social cohesion.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Logistical Synchronization}}}$: Unified voter lists and shared polling stations would simplify the work of the **Election Commission of India (Art 324)**.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Economic Productivity}}}$: Frequent shutdowns and rallies during elections impact local trade and daily wage earners; ONOE minimizes this disruption.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Global Precedent}}}$: Countries like South Africa and Sweden follow a similar model, showing that it is possible in a democratic framework.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Kovind Committee (2024)}}}$, **Recommendations**: The committee highlighted that ONOE could boost GDP by 1.5% by reducing inflationary electoral spending.
  - $\color{#7be382}{\textsf{\textbf{Infrastructure Utilization}}}$: Schools and public buildings would be occupied for election purposes much less frequently.

- **Conclusion:**
  - ONOE is a "Reform for Efficiency." While it requires complex constitutional amendments, its potential to transform India from a "Campaigning State" to a "Governing State" makes it a reform worth pursuing through broad consensus.
