### Q1. Write down a comphrehensive list of acts passed in last 10 years.

The last ten years (2016–2026) have seen monumental legislative transformations in India, reshaping criminal justice, taxation, digital privacy, and the business landscape.

The most significant acts passed by the Parliament of India over the past decade are organized by domain below:

### Criminal Justice & Security Reforms

* **The Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), 2023:** Replaced the Indian Penal Code (IPC) of 1860, streamlining offenses, introducing community service as punishment, and updating provisions around terrorism and organized crime.
* **The Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (BNSS), 2023:** Replaced the Code of Criminal Procedure (CrPC), modernizing procedures by enforcing timelines for investigations and trials, and prioritizing digitization.
* **The Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam (BSA), 2023:** Replaced the Indian Evidence Act of 1872, broadening the scope of electronic and digital evidence.
* **The Public Examinations (Prevention of Unfair Means) Act, 2024:** Enacted to curb paper leaks and cheating in public examinations with stringent penalties.
* **The Unlawful Activities (Prevention) Amendment Act, 2019:** Empowered the central government to designate individual suspects as terrorists (previously only organizations could be designated).

### Economy, Taxation & Corporate Governance

* **The Goods and Services Tax (GST) Act, 2017:** Unified the complex indirect tax structure into a single national framework under the principle of "One Nation, One Tax."
* **The Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC), 2016:** Consolidated existing frameworks to introduce a time-bound, market-driven process for resolving corporate insolvency.
* **The Fugitive Economic Offenders Act, 2018:** Allows authorities to confiscate properties and economic assets of economic offenders who flee the country to evade prosecution.
* **The Labor Codes (2019–2020):** Consolidated 29 central labor laws into 4 comprehensive codes: the *Code on Wages*, *Industrial Relations Code*, *Social Security Code*, and *Occupational Safety, Health and Working Conditions Code*.
* **The Income-tax Act, 2025:** Comprehensively reviewed and overhauled the older 1961 law to make tax legislation modern, concise, and easier to comprehend.

### Technology, Media & Connectivity

* **The Digital Personal Data Protection (DPDP) Act, 2023:** Established India's first comprehensive statutory framework for processing personal data, protecting digital privacy rights while establishing data fiduciary obligations.
* **The Telecommunications Act, 2023:** Replaced the colonial-era Indian Telegraph Act (1885), modernizing the regulatory layout for telecom networks, spectrum allocations, and national security provisions.
* **The Jan Vishwas (Amendment of Provisions) Act, 2023 & 2026:** Decriminalized minor offenses across dozens of central legislations to reduce judicial pendency and boost the ease of doing business.

### Polity, Governance & Civil Rights

* **The Constitution (106th Amendment) Act, 2023 (Nari Shakti Vandan Adhiniyam):** Provided a 33% reservation for women in the Lok Sabha and State Legislative Assemblies.
* **The Constitution (103rd Amendment) Act, 2019:** Introduced a 10% reservation for the Economically Weaker Sections (EWS) in admissions and public employment.
* **The Citizenship (Amendment) Act, 2019:** Amended the 1955 Act to grant an accelerated path to citizenship for persecuted religious minorities from Pakistan, Bangladesh, and Afghanistan who entered India before 2015.
* **The Jammu and Kashmir Reorganisation Act, 2019:** Bifurcated the erstwhile state of Jammu and Kashmir into two distinct Union Territories: Jammu & Kashmir, and Ladakh.

### Social Welfare, Healthcare & Environment

* **The Rights of Persons with Disabilities (RPwD) Act, 2016:** Expanded the number of recognized disabilities from 7 to 21, securing dynamic access to public infrastructure, employment quotas, and education.
* **The Muslim Women (Protection of Rights on Marriage) Act, 2019:** Formally criminalized the practice of instant vocal or written divorce (*Triple Talaq*).
* **The Consumer Protection Act, 2019:** Replaced the 1986 law to introduce stricter liability rules, cover e-commerce transactions, and set up the Central Consumer Protection Authority (CCPA).
* **The Mental Healthcare Act, 2017:** Decriminalized suicide attempts under the older penal frameworks and explicitly recognized the right to mental healthcare access.
* **The Energy Conservation (Amendment) Act, 2022:** Paved the path for a domestic carbon credit trading market and mandated the utilization of non-fossil sources for energy needs.
